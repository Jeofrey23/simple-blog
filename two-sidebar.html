Continuation...The Historic Significance...

As I continued my walk, I reflected on the history of the bridge. Originally built in 2006, it has withstood typhoons. The bridge’s ability to adapt and endure speaks volumes about the ingenuity behind its design. Also with it's benefit with the residents which is a great shortcut that lessen the travel time where residents walk from the rivers .

Petwa-guey Bridge

My Final Thoughts

Crossing this bridge was more than just a physical journey; it felt like stepping into a different era. It’s not just a bridge; it’s a living monument to the past, present, and future. As I reached the end and turned back to look at it one last time, I felt a sense of awe and gratitude. This experience was a reminder of how bridges do more than just connect places—they connect us to the stories, people, and moments that shape our world.

If you ever find yourself in Camanpaguey Cabitin, I highly recommend making the Lub-ban,Petwa-guey, and Nansubli bridges a part of your journey. Whether you’re walking across or simply pausing to admire it, the experience is one that will stay with you long after you leave.
